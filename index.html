How to be a freelance writer

Assembly time: indefinetly!
How certain am I this will work out? 0%

likelyhood you'll cry at some point?: 100%

I have no clue what I'm doing, but when has that ever stopped anyone from giving unsolicited advice?

  • Things You'll Need

  • a lot of patience
  • a good place to cry
  • waaaaay more time than you think
  • probably wine, put in crying spot
  • a friend to vent to
  • Water

  1. You just have to start writing

    I know what you may be thinking, "but I have nothing to write!" or "well nobody cares about the things I want to write about!" look, there are YouTubers making millions of dollars from filming themselves playing video games, and if they can turn their passion into a career, so can you!

  2. Tell your friends about your writing

    Yeah I know, that's not a very "cool, manic pixie dream girl" thing to do, but part of being a writer is writing a lot of embarassing things a lot of times (example: this webpage) until one day, it's not anymore and all of a sudden it is cool! Trust me on this ok?!?!?!

    I learned how to add a photo, but then couldn't figure out how to change the photo, so I guess this is pretty acccurate.

  3. Write what matters to you

    Here's the thing, if you care about it, someone else does too. For instance, I LOVE public transportation, and I will literally talk to anyone who will listen about it, and truthfully, a lot of people will! So here's a video about public transit because it's important, and you've already made it this far into the list, might as well take a fun reading break and watch something informative.

  4. Drink water! Or wine! Both! Eat a cookie! Cry a little!

    I don't know I just feel like people are taught to not care about things, or downplay how important something is to them, especially with creative things, so if you wanted to hear someone say it's ok to wallow in self-pity every now and then, I'm giving you permission!

  5. Now get back up, try again and THAT'S IT!!!!!

    Now here's where all the boomer-y advice comes in, "pull yourself up by the bootstraps!" "Whatever happened to hard work in this country?!" "People just don't want to work anymore!" yadayadayadayada I know I know, but anyhow if you want to be a writer, you just have to keep starting over, and over, and over.... and over. But I promise! Someday everything is all going to work out just fine! For real!
